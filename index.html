Report Narrative

⚠️ Professional Report Writing Standards — click to expand ▼
Reports must be court-ready and stand alone without officer testimony. Include ALL critical details:
  • No police codes — Say "traffic stop" not "Code 4". Say "traffic collision" not "Code 12". Say "officer in distress" not "Code 9". Say "silent alarm activation" not "Code 15". Location codes (VIN 900, PC 384) and station codes (MEL 900, MEL 258) are acceptable.
  • No informal language — Say "incapacitated the suspect" not "laced him". Say "discharged their firearm" not "shot him". Say "rendered first aid" not "did medical". The report is a legal document.
  • Scene on arrival — What did you see, hear, or observe upon arriving? Number of individuals, vehicles present, weapons visible, injuries already sustained.
  • Identification method — State exactly how the suspect was identified: driver's licence, fingerprint scanner, MDT profile, DNA match, or verbal confirmation. Include on every report.
  • Force justification — Always state the threat BEFORE the response. "The suspect produced a firearm and aimed at officers — [CALLSIGN] discharged their service weapon, incapacitating the suspect." Never just state force was used without the reason.
  • Medical & transport — Who rendered first aid? Was MAS/AV called and did they attend? Was the suspect transported to hospital before processing? Which station was the suspect processed at (VIN 900, MEL 900, FKN station)?
  • Pursuit specifics — Approximate duration, route/direction, how it ended (vehicle breakdown, boxed in, voluntary stop, crash, abandoned). Note any vehicle swaps, dangerous driving, or speeds reached.
  • Weapons & serial numbers — Type, quantity, and serial number for every weapon (e.g. 1x Carbine Rifle SN: 667056QNR256337). Include ammo type and count.
  • Victims & hostages — How many? Names if known. Injuries sustained. Demands made. How were they released?
  • Vehicle details — Make/model/colour and registration. Include MELROADS extract for warrants and impounds. Note if vehicle was stolen or registered to the suspect.
  • Subject behaviour — Compliant or resistant? Threats made? Statements or admissions? Lawyer requested? Behaviour during processing?
  • Callsigns with names — Use radio ID with name on first mention: "POR 431 SC Joseph O'Connelley". Callsign only for subsequent references.
  • Exact locations — Use PC numbers (PC 584, PC 220), station codes, and specific areas (rooftop, eastern side, canal, Del Perro Freeway).
  • Caution & processing — Note that caution was read and whether the suspect understood/accepted. Note where processed and where sentenced (MRC, Bolingbroke).
  • Chain of events — Use transitions: "Upon arrival…", "While moving to assist…", "Subsequently…", "Following this…", "At that time…"
  • Timeline — Key times throughout: arrival, pursuit start/end, apprehension, processing, sentencing.
  • Sentence approvals — Note approving officer inline: "Approved by SGT X" or "Self-Approved — SGT+". Include if lawyer negotiated a reduced sentence.
